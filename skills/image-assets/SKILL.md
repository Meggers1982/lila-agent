# Image Assets Skill

## When to Use This Skill
Read this file before proposing visual directions or generating any image. It is the authoritative reference for format specs and aesthetic registers. Do not rely on memory.

---

## Format Catalog

| Format | Dimensions | Use Case |
|---|---:|---|
| **1:1 square** | 1080×1080 | Instagram feed, LinkedIn feed, multi-platform safe |
| **4:5 portrait** | 1080×1350 | Instagram feed portrait, paid social, product launch posts |
| **9:16 story/reel cover** | 1080×1920 | Stories, Reels covers, TikTok covers, vertical launch frames |
| **1.91:1 landscape** | 1200×628 | LinkedIn link posts, Meta ads, X/Twitter cards |
| **16:9 landscape** | 1920×1080 | YouTube thumbnails, presentation headers, website hero stills |
| **Carousel** | 1080×1350 or 1080×1080 | Multi-slide education, feature proof, launch narrative |

### Layout Rules by Format

**9:16 (Story/Reel):** Strong vertical hierarchy. CTA near lower third. Safe margins for app UI chrome (at least 250px top and bottom). One focal element.

**4:5 (Portrait):** Feed-first hero composition. Bold but not cramped. High-value product detail reads before copy. Do not center everything.

**1:1 (Square):** Balanced composition, one clear focal point. Copy must be very short — maximum one short sentence or a headline.

**1.91:1 / 16:9 (Landscape):** Horizontal composition. Avoid center-only design. Leave room for cropped link previews on the right or left.

**Carousel:** Each slide must work standalone and in sequence. Slide 1 earns the swipe. Last slide has the CTA.

## Aesthetic Registers

Pick one primary register per project. Adapt it to the brand. Do not default.

### Editorial Restraint
**For:** Heritage brands, craft goods, wellness, thoughtful consumer products, premium services.
**Typography:** Serif + mono or refined sans. EB Garamond, Playfair Display, JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex.
**Palette:** Warm neutrals, deep ink, one earthy accent. Never more than three colors.
**Imagery:** Tactile detail, natural light, restrained product presence. Negative space is intentional.
**Copy:** Quiet, specific, no hype. One claim per image.
**Avoid:** Bright gradients, busy backgrounds, centered text stacks.

### Tech Minimal
**For:** SaaS, AI tools, developer tools, hardware, productivity, B2B.
**Typography:** Tight sans + mono. Outfit, IBM Plex Sans/Mono, JetBrains Mono. Inter only when deliberately utility-driven.
**Palette:** Neutral base (white, off-white, or dark) with one high-signal accent. No rainbow palettes.
**Imagery:** Clean product/UI moments, real workflow context, precise diagrams. No abstract tech metaphors.
**Copy:** Direct claim, concrete benefit, quantified proof when available. Numbers beat adjectives.
**Avoid:** Glowing particles, circuit boards, floating UI fragments, neon on dark.

### Brand Distinct
**For:** Fashion, beauty, lifestyle, premium drops, creator brands, limited editions.
**Typography:** Expressive display + restrained support face. Custom or distinctive type is a feature.
**Palette:** Saturated brand colors, confident contrast. Monochromatic is fine. Avoid muted-everything.
**Imagery:** Full-bleed, styled, editorial, ownable. Not stock. Cast and setting are brand signals.
**Copy:** Declarative, attitude-rich, short. The image carries the weight; copy punctuates it.
**Avoid:** Generic lifestyle photography, soft pastel safety, over-explaining.

### Playful Warm
**For:** Food, family, hospitality, wellness, everyday consumer goods, community brands.
**Typography:** Friendly sans + soft serif. Rounded letterforms. Nunito, DM Sans, Lora.
**Palette:** Cream, warm accents, soft contrast, coordinated multi-color (max four). Never harsh.
**Imagery:** Lived-in, bright, human, textured. Real environments, not styled studios.
**Copy:** Conversational and specific. Write like a person, not a brand.
**Avoid:** Cold whites, corporate type, empty space that reads as sterile.

### Cinematic Dark
**For:** Luxury goods, automotive, spirits, watches, prestige tech, night launches, high-end fashion.
**Typography:** Sharp serif or refined sans. Canela, Freight Display, Söhne.
**Palette:** Tinted blacks, metal, gold/copper/silver, one accent. Never more than three tones.
**Imagery:** Shadow, rim light, dramatic product reveal, controlled negative space. Reveal > expose.
**Copy:** Few words, high confidence. A product name and a year is enough.
**Avoid:** Bright backgrounds, busy layouts, soft or pastel anything.

## Extended Asset Types

Use these for non-product briefs: events, thought leadership, personal brand, educational content, community posts.

- **Quote card:** A single compelling statement, attributed or standalone. Typography is the hero — the layout, type size, and weight do the work. One background treatment. No decorative clutter. Works best 1:1 or 4:5.
- **Thought leadership post:** Opinion, insight, data point, or hot take formatted for feed. Pull-quote or editorial layout. Copy-forward — the image serves the words, not the other way around.
- **Event announcement:** Name, date, and location in a clear hierarchy. Urgency without noise. Include only what someone needs to decide whether to attend. Works at 1:1 and 4:5.
- **Infographic tile:** One stat, one comparison, or one process step — not a full infographic. A single scannable data moment. Use when a number or contrast is the whole story.
- **Educational carousel:** Teach one thing across 4–6 slides. Slide 1 = the hook or question. Middle slides = steps, proof, or examples. Last slide = the takeaway + CTA. Each slide must work standalone.
- **Community or culture post:** Celebration, milestone, team moment, or values statement. Human-first, not brand-first. Warmer register, less production polish.
- **Personal brand post:** Individual person as subject — speaking announcement, press feature, launch of their own project. Name and role are the anchor. Tone matches the person, not a company.
- **Newsletter or content cover:** Header image for a newsletter issue, blog post, podcast episode, or YouTube video. Title hierarchy is the primary design element.

**Register guidance for non-product briefs:**
- Quote cards and thought leadership → Editorial Restraint or Tech Minimal
- Events and announcements → Brand Distinct or Playful Warm depending on tone
- Educational carousels → Tech Minimal (data-heavy) or Playful Warm (approachable)
- Community posts → Playful Warm
- Personal brand → match the individual's voice; Editorial Restraint is the safe default
- Prestige events or luxury brand moments → Cinematic Dark

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do NOT use all five asset types for every brief. Choose what has a real job.
- Do NOT mix two aesthetic registers in the same set. One register, adapted.
- Do NOT put long copy on the image itself. Move it to the caption.
- Do NOT generate without first searching for real-world visual references.
- Do NOT skip the asset board approval step. Ever.
- Do NOT pick a format without checking the layout rules above first.

## Validation Checklist (run before saving each asset)

- [ ] Dimensions match the format spec in the catalog above
- [ ] Text overlay is readable at mobile size (simulate a 375px wide screen)
- [ ] Palette matches the approved direction (not more than three colors unless Playful Warm)
- [ ] File name follows: `<brand>-<campaign>-<asset>-<format>.png`
- [ ] Alt text written for this specific asset
- [ ] Caption option written for this specific asset
